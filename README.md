# Abstraction, Polymorphism, Inheritance, Encapsulation

The core concepts of OOP relies on its four principles, <i>Abstraction, Polymorphism, Inheritance, Encapsulation</i>

- Abstraction

Allows us to hide and reveal complex data or processes. 

There are two types of abstraction: 

1. Data Abstraction - to hide complex data and use (or reveal) what we want. 

2. Process Abstraction -  to hide complex processes where we break down systems into manageable and small parts. 

- Polymorphism 

To override or overload methods provided by a superclass.

- Inheritance 

To extend a parent class to its child class.

- Encapsulation 

To use access modifiers, <code>private</code> and <code>public</code> or default.
